Value Betting

Mathematics Instead of Guessing

A value bet is a wager on a sports event where the bookmaker offers an overvalued odds - meaning the probability of the event outcome according to your analysis is higher than reflected in the bookmaker's odds.

But let's be honest. How many of you can actually understand where the odds are overvalued and where they're not? Probably not many. So why guess when value betting trends can be tracked across thousands of already played matches?

How to Find a Value Bet?

Bookmakers consider many factors when setting odds: statistics, injuries, motivation. Their resources far exceed the capabilities of an ordinary bettor.

Our approach is based on historical data analysis. The database contains thousands of matches played under the same odds offered today. We look for statistical patterns, not try to predict the future.

The key point - we see trends. When under certain odds the same outcome regularly occurs, this is already a statistical pattern.

The Trap of Clear Favorites

Maybe you want to know how a clear favorite plays at home in England. A clear favorite is when the opening odds for the favorite are below 1.25. Everyone loves betting on clear favorites: Barcelona, Inter, PSG, Liverpool, Bayern.

Statistics of clear favorites (odds ≤ 1.25) in EPL over last 2 seasons

And what do we see. The clear favorite won 17 matches out of 20, but we would incur losses if we bet on them because the odds given for clear favorites are too low. And it's enough for them to not win just 3 times and this nullifies all their 17 wins.

Conclusion: Even with 85% win rate, bets on such favorites are unprofitable due to low odds. This is a classic example where intuition fails and mathematics shows the real picture.

Total Goals Betting

We all love when many goals are scored in a match, and often bet on Over 2.5 goals. For example, let's take the most scoring league in Europe - Bundesliga, where on average 3.19 goals are scored per match. Let's take opening odds for Over 2.5 in the range of 1.60 - 1.69. There were 97 matches total, and we would get a profit of 1.98 times our stake.

ROI for Over 2.5 with opening odds 1.60 - 1.69 is 1.98

But let's see what would happen if we bet on the same range, but by closing odds. There were 58 such matches, and we would get a profit of 11.12 times our stake, which is 5.5 times more.

ROI for Over 2.5 with closing odds 1.60 - 1.69 is 11.12

Conclusion

Value betting is a mathematically justified strategy for long-term earnings. Key principles:

  1. Distance matters more than single wins - statistics work on large samples
  2. Trust numbers, not intuition - emotions interfere with objective assessment
  3. Manage your bankroll - without proper money management any strategy loses
  4. Use historical data - past matches show future trends
